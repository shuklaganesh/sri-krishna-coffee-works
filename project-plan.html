Vendor Coordination Plan

Standup Pouch Vendor - Critical Information Needed

Technical Specifications to Request ▼
  • Exact die-line template (.AI or .PDF with layers)
  • Degassing valve specifications:
    • Type (one-way valve vs. micro-perforations)
    • Exact placement coordinates
    • No-print zones around vent
    • Installation method and timing
    • Food-grade certification
  • Material specifications:
    • Laminate structure (BOPP/Metalized/PE recommended for coffee)
    • Barrier properties (aroma retention specs)
    • UV protection capabilities
    • Moisture barrier specifications
  • Resealable zipper details (type and placement)
  • Printing specifications:
    • File format (print-ready PDF requirements)
    • Color management (CMYK, Pantone matching process)
    • Bleed requirements (typically 3mm)
    • Safe text zones (minimum 5mm from trim)
    • Resolution requirements (300 DPI minimum)
    • Proofing process (digital and/or physical)
Production Requirements ▼
  • Minimum Order Quantity (MOQ) - expect 500-1,000 units
  • Pricing tiers (500, 1,000, 2,500, 5,000 units)
  • Lead time from approval to delivery
  • Sample pouch availability and cost
  • Payment terms (advance %, balance on delivery)
  • Revision policy and associated costs
Vendor Evaluation Checklist ▼
  • Request physical samples of previous work
  • Check food-grade certifications
  • Verify FSSAI compliance knowledge
  • Assess responsiveness and communication
  • Compare quotes from 2-3 vendors
  • Check references from other food brands
  • Confirm technical support during design phase
Template Questions for Vendor ▼
  1. "Can you provide the exact die-line template with degassing valve placement marked?"
  2. "What is your recommended laminate structure for coffee packaging to preserve aroma?"
  3. "Do you offer design review services to ensure print-ready files?"
  4. "What is your MOQ and price break structure?"
  5. "Can you provide a sample pouch (blank or with previous client design)?"
  6. "What is your typical lead time from file approval to delivery?"
  7. "Do you assist with FSSAI labeling compliance?"

Risk Management

Production Risks

Risk: Vendor print quality issues or color mismatch

Final pouches may not match approved design specifications.

Mitigation: Approve physical proof before full run; include reprinting clause in vendor contract
Risk: MOQ too high for initial budget

Vendor minimum order quantities may exceed financial capacity.

Mitigation: Get quotes from 2-3 vendors; negotiate or consider starting with smaller format if needed
Risk: Production delays impact launch timeline

Manufacturing or shipping delays could push back launch date.

Mitigation: Build 2-week buffer; start e-commerce setup in parallel; soft launch possible without full inventory

Budget Risks

Risk: Vendor quotes exceed estimates

Packaging production costs may be higher than budgeted.

Mitigation: 15-20% contingency budget; prioritize essentials; phased approach (launch with one SKU)
Risk: Hidden costs in e-commerce setup

Apps, plugins, transaction fees may add unexpected expenses.

Mitigation: Research all costs upfront; use free alternatives where possible; factor in 2.5% transaction fees

Timeline Risks

Risk: Design revisions take longer than expected

Multiple revision rounds could extend timeline.

Mitigation: Clear revision limits (3 rounds per Khushi's quote); approve "good enough" vs. perfect
Risk: Vendor capacity issues delay production

Vendor may not have capacity to meet timeline.

Mitigation: Confirm vendor capacity before committing; identify backup vendor

Quality Risks

Risk: Package doesn't preserve coffee freshness

Valve failure or inadequate barrier properties could affect product quality.

Mitigation: Test sample pouches with actual coffee; 2-week freshness test before full launch
Risk: E-commerce site has bugs or poor user experience

Technical issues could frustrate customers and reduce conversions.

Mitigation: Thorough testing phase; soft launch to friends/family first; gather feedback
